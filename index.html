Fascinating Facts About Romania

The heaviest building in the world is in Romania

According to the Guinness World Records the heaviest building in the world is in Romania.The Palace of Parliament in Bucharest contains 700,000 tonnes of steel and bronze, 1,000,000 square feet of marble, 3500 tonnes of crystal, and 900,000 square meters of wood.

The first European city to have electric street lights is in Romania

Timisoara in Romania, was the first one to have street lights installed in 1884.

Romania has a perfectly round-shaped village

Just 40 km away from Timisoara is the only circular village in Romania: Charlottenburg (or Sarlota).The houses are built in a radial way around a plaza, at even distances from each other. However, the perfectly round shape is obvious only from above.

Romania is home to the tallest wooden church in the world

he church tower of the Sapînta-Peri Monastery in the region of Maramures measures 78 meters in height and it has a 7 meter-high cross.

The modern jet engine was invented by a Romanian

Henri Coanda was a Romanian aerodynamics pioneer and engineer. In 1910 he developed an experimental aircraft that used the world’s first jet engine. During the time of the 2nd World War, there have been many different inventors who tried to perfect the original model of the jet engine invented by Henri Coanda. Thanks to Coanda’s inventions and discoveries, flying is possible today.

There is a Romanian Version of ‘Mount Rushmore’ on the Danube River

arved in the rocky bank of the Danube River, near the city of Orsova, is the tallest rock sculpture in Europe, the statue of Dacian King Decebal (135 feet tall).

Romania has its own Arc de Triomphe

es, it’s true, Romania has its own Triumphal Arch (Arcul de Triumf) and the interesting fact is that it’s an identical replica of Arc de Triomphe de l’Étoile in Paris.

Ciucas Mountains

A small massif nestled in the Carpathians Bend. the Ciucaș Mountains are considered part of the Curvature Carpathians. Geologically, according to the divisions of the Carpathians, the range is part of the Moldavian-Muntenian Carpathians, of the larger group of the Outer Eastern Carpathians.
